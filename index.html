Research Interests

A core aspect of my approach is data-driven mathematical analysis: I use large-scale computational experiments (Sage, Python) to identify patterns in algebraic objects, fit models to observed behavior, and formulate conjectures that can later be justified theoretically. This workflow mirrors many problems in the real world, where structure must be inferred from data before being formalized.

Favorite Texts & Influences

My mathematical interests have been shaped by a mix of classical texts and research-oriented exposition that emphasize explicit structure, concrete examples, and conceptual clarity. I am particularly drawn to works that bridge abstract theory with computable objects and applications.

  • J.-P. Serre, A Course in Arithmetic — for its elegant and concise treatment of cyclotomic fields, characters, and foundational ideas in algebraic number theory.
  • J. H. Silverman and J. Tate, Rational Points on Elliptic Curves — for its clear, example-driven introduction to elliptic curves, Diophantine equations, and the arithmetic geometry underlying modern cryptography.
  • D. A. Marcus, Number Fields — for its accessible yet rigorous development of algebraic number theory, with an emphasis on explicit calculations and concrete intuition.
